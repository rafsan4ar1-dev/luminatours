The experience

Witness the stars, and be photographed among them

Away from Queenstown's lights, the night sky over the Southern Alps is among the darkest and clearest anywhere on Earth. On a moonless night the Milky Way arches from one horizon to the other, with the Southern Cross, bright planets, and thousands of stars revealed, the kind of sky most people never get to see.

This is your chance to stand beneath it. Your photographer-guide takes you well away from the lights, helps you find your way around the night sky, and photographs you among the stars, a chance to capture your place in the cosmos.

(Best from June to August.)

The details

What's included

Duration
2 to 3 hours

Your evening is private and unhurried, with everything taken care of. You're collected from your Queenstown accommodation and driven to a secret dark-sky spot, where your guide points out the constellations and the stories behind them, no experience needed. There's time to simply take it in, often with a warm drink in hand, while you're photographed beneath the stars. A few days later, a curated set of professionally edited night-sky images arrives, yours to keep.

The evening

A typical itinerary

A private pickup from your Queenstown accommodation, timed to the Milky Way's best visibility and the night's sky conditions.

Travel in comfort to one of several secret locations, chosen for moon phase, cloud cover and season, for the darkest, clearest skies.

A short orientation to the major constellations, the Southern Cross and the Milky Way, with the basics of night-sky photography.

Your guide captures a series of images of you under the night sky, from wide Milky Way landscapes to creative starlit portraits.

A peaceful moment under the stars with an optional warm drink, taking in the silence and depth of the southern sky.

A relaxed drive back. Within a few days, a curated selection of professionally edited images arrives, your keepsake of the night.
